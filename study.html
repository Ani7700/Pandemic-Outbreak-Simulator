A study of interactive tools for vaccination-coverage information

Participant Information Sheet

Thank you for your interest. Please read this carefully before deciding whether to take part. The study evaluates how different ways of showing the same risk numbers help people read them accurately. It is completed online and takes about 30–40 minutes. You will use an interactive tool three times, each time with a different (fictional) disease and a different display style, and answer short comprehension questions after each.

All data shown is fictional. The diseases (“Verrow fever”, “Solvik”, “Tarnpox”), the vaccine, and every statistic — coverage, cases, and risks — are entirely hypothetical, used only so we can measure how you read the information, not your knowledge of any real disease. The areas and map are real UK locations, used only so the tool works. Nothing here is a prediction about any real disease, vaccine, or area, and nothing should be used for real health decisions. For accurate vaccination information, consult the NHS (www.nhs.uk) or your GP.

The study’s only focus is how accurately you understand the information displayed, and how usable you find it. No questions are asked about your views, feelings, intentions, decisions, or trust regarding vaccination, and no personally identifying information is collected. We record only your answers to comprehension questions, usability ratings, and the time taken; these are stored anonymously.

Taking part is voluntary. You may withdraw at any time without giving a reason, and may request deletion of your data up to two weeks after your session.

Organised by Ninghan Qi and Dr Miri Zilka, Department of Engineering, University of Cambridge (PI: Dr Miri Zilka). Results form part of an MPhil dissertation and may be published for groups of participants; you will not be identified. Reviewed by the Department of Engineering Research Ethics Committee.
